Degeneracy in EMRI

In the future (around 2030s), a space-based gravitational wave detector, LISA, will be launched. In its sensitive band (mHz), extreme-mass-ratio inspirals (EMRIs) will be major sources of signal. One ambitious goal of LISA is, using EMRI signal, to map the spacetime around the supermassive black holes. However, even with perfect data in hand, some issue may hinder us when doing this. One of them is the degeneracy between signals from Kerr metric (paradign for description of black holes) and that from generic metric beyond Kerr. This work was published on Physical Review D. Changing-look quasar

Quasars used to be mysterious objects for a long time. When met with myterious objects, people tend to do classification like botanists. A famous classification is Seyfert I/II and intermediate types. Some people may think we have fully understood them by the unified model for acative galactic nuclei (AGN). But recently some observations show that quasars can change their types in a short period of time (weeks or months). We analyzed the data for the object SDSS J141324+530527 in Sloan Digital Sky Survey (SDSS) Reverberation Mapping project. This work was published on the Astrophysical Journal.
